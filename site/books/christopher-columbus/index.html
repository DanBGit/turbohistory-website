Christopher Columbus

The Fast, Sharp Story

A cheese merchant's son who couldn't do math correctly changed the course of human history - by accident.

In 1492, Christopher Columbus set sail to prove a shortcut to Asia existed. He was wrong about the distance, wrong about what he'd find, and wrong about almost everything that followed. But his four voyages across the Atlantic slammed two hemispheres together with consequences still echoing today.

Inside this hour-long read:

  • How a Genoese port kid went from hawking cheese to pitching kings (and getting rejected for doing the math wrong)
  • The unlikely alliance with Spain that funded his first voyage - after years of being told no
  • What really happened when he landed in the Bahamas thinking it was the East Indies
  • The darker turn: enslavement, brutality, and a governor so corrupt Spain threw him in chains
  • Why he died still believing he'd reached Asia, never knowing he'd stumbled onto two continents

No romantic myths. No noble explorer nonsense. Just the strange, brutal, world-splitting story of a man whose greatest discovery was a colossal mistake.
